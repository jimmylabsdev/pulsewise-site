What it does

01

Voice-first capture

Just say your reading. PulseWise extracts the systolic, diastolic, and pulse — no typing, no fiddling. Works with natural phrasing like "one twenty over eighty, pulse seventy-two", and manual entry is always one tap away.

02

Private by design

Every reading lives on your phone. No accounts, no cloud sync, no analytics, no ads. Voice transcripts are processed in memory and discarded the moment your numbers come back.

03

Doctor-ready exports

Generate clean PDF or CSV reports of your history in seconds. Share with your physician, keep for your records, or back up however you like. Your data, your file.

Why I built this

"I was recently diagnosed with severe hypertension. The first few weeks were rough — blurred vision, sudden spikes, the body announcing itself in ways it never had before. Medication has settled most of it, though the spikes still come without warning. So my doctor asked me to monitor my BP every day. It's become as routine as brushing my teeth — the first thing I do in the morning, and the last before bed.

I started with a notebook, but it got messy fast. So I went looking for an app. Most drowned me in ads — pop-ups between readings, banners under the chart, full-screen takeovers at 6 a.m. when all I wanted was to spend two quiet minutes with my own health.

So I built PulseWise. A quiet notebook for my numbers. I added voice entry because typing one-handed at dawn has its own kind of struggle.

Like me, if you're tracking your BP too - or know someone who does (a parent, a partner, a friend) - I hope PulseWise becomes a small, calm part of the day.

Hypertension runs quietly through most of our families in India.

Pass this along to anyone who might need it."

A small disclaimer: PulseWise is not a medical device. It's a quiet place to keep your numbers and notice what's changing. Always consult your doctor.
