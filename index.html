What it does

01

Voice-first capture

Just say your reading. PulseWise extracts the systolic, diastolic, and pulse — no typing, no fiddling. Works with natural phrasing like "one twenty over eighty, pulse seventy-two", and manual entry is always one tap away.

02

Private by design

Every reading lives on your phone. No accounts, no cloud sync, no analytics, no ads. Voice transcripts are processed in memory and discarded the moment your numbers come back.

03

Doctor-ready exports

Generate clean PDF or CSV reports of your history in seconds. Share with your physician, keep for your records, or back up however you like. Your data, your file.

Why I built this

"I have hypertension. At its peak my blood pressure once read 280 over 260. Since then, monitoring has become part of how I think about every day — morning and evening, without fail.

I tried the existing apps. They kept pushing premium subscriptions when I just needed to write down a number. The free ones drowned me in ads — pop-ups between readings, banners under the chart, full-screen takeovers when I opened the app to log a 6 a.m. measurement. None of them felt like a place I wanted to spend two minutes a day with my own health.

So I built PulseWise. No accounts. No ads. No subscriptions. Just a quiet notebook for your numbers, with voice entry because typing one-handed at dawn is its own kind of friction. It's the app I needed. I hope it's useful to you too."

PulseWise is not a medical device. It's a quiet place to keep your numbers and notice what's changing. Always consult your doctor.
